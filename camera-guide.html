Camera Permission Guide

Follow these simple steps to allow camera access on your phone for seamless attendance check-in. Takes less than 1 minute!

⚡ Quick Fix — Try This First!

Before going through the full steps, try these quick fixes:

1

Look for the Camera Popup

When you click "Check-In", your browser will show a popup asking "Allow camera access?". Tap "Allow".

2

If You Accidentally Blocked It

Tap the lock icon 🔒 (or ⓘ) in your browser's address bar → Find "Camera" → Change to "Allow" → Reload the page.

3

Close Other Camera Apps

If another app (WhatsApp Video, Instagram, etc.) is using the camera, close it first, then try again.

✅ Still not working? Follow the detailed steps below for your phone type (Android or iPhone).

📱 Detailed Steps by Phone Type

🤖 Android Phone — Google Chrome

1

Open Chrome Browser

Open Google Chrome on your Android phone. Tap the three dots (⋮) menu at the top-right corner.

2

Go to Settings → Site Settings

In the menu, tap Settings → Scroll down and tap Site Settings.

3

Find Camera Option

Inside Site Settings, find and tap Camera. You will see the camera permission toggle.

4

Allow Camera Access

Make sure the toggle is ON (blue). If our website is listed under "Blocked", tap on it and change to "Allow".

⚠️ Also check: Go to your phone's Settings → Apps → Chrome → Permissions → Camera → Allow. Sometimes the app-level permission needs to be enabled separately.

🍎 iPhone — Safari Browser

1

Open iPhone Settings

Go to your iPhone's home screen and open the Settings app (⚙️ gear icon).

2

Find Safari

Scroll down in Settings and tap Safari.

3

Open Camera Settings

Scroll down in Safari settings to find "Settings for Websites" section. Tap Camera.

4

Select "Allow"

Change the camera setting to "Allow". This will let Safari access your camera for attendance photo.

⚠️ Using Chrome on iPhone? Go to Settings → Chrome → Camera → Allow.

💻 Desktop / Laptop — Chrome Browser

1

Click the Lock/Info Icon

In Chrome's address bar, click the lock icon (🔒) or ⓘ icon on the left side of the URL.

2

Find Camera Permission

A dropdown will appear. Find "Camera" in the list of permissions.

3

Change to "Allow"

Click the dropdown next to Camera and select "Allow".

4

Reload the Page

Refresh the page by pressing Ctrl + R (or Cmd + R on Mac). Now try checking in again.

🌐 Alternative: Web Check-In (No Camera Needed)

If your camera is still not working, you can use the "🌐 Web Check-In" button on your Employee Dashboard. This will mark your attendance without a photo, using your IP address for location verification.

⚠️ Important: Web Check-In is only for emergency use when the camera is not functioning. Your admin can see which check-ins were done via web vs camera. Always try camera check-in first.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the app need my camera?
The camera captures your photo during check-in/check-out to verify your identity and prevent proxy attendance. Your photo is securely stored and only visible to you and your admin.
I allowed the camera but it's still showing black screen
Try these: 1) Close all other apps using the camera (WhatsApp video, Instagram). 2) Restart your browser. 3) Restart your phone. 4) Clear browser cache: Settings → Chrome → Clear Data.
My phone says "No camera found"
Some older phones or budget phones may have camera compatibility issues with web browsers. In this case, use the "🌐 Web Check-In" button from your dashboard sidebar as an alternative.
Is my location tracked during check-in?
Yes, your GPS location (or IP-based location) is captured along with your photo for attendance records. This helps verify you are at the office or an approved work location.
Can I check in from home using Web Check-In?
Web Check-In logs your IP address and approximate location. Your admin can see this data. Using it from an unauthorized location without approval may be flagged.
